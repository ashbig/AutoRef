CC   Construction of shuttle plasmids with pST76K_SR
CC   The plasmid replicates well only at 30-32°C.
CC   Positive selection: 	50µg/ml kanamycin
CC   Negative selection: 	5-10% sucrose
CC   IPTG (might be necessary, because the promotor of sacB was a PlacZ
CC   and sometimes reverts to a repressable one)
CC   The mutation (deletion, insertion, point mutant) should be flanked
CC   by targeting sequences 500-1000 bp in length.
CC   All shuttle plasmids should be tested for temperature sensitive
CC   replication (should not grow at 41-43 deg C) and sucrose sensitivity
CC   - should be inhibited for growth on 5-10% sucrose LB plates.
CC   Generation of cointegrates:
CC   electroporation (1.25-1.8 kV; 200Ohm; 25µF; 4.5-5.0 ms; 0.1cm
CC   cuvette; 50 µl electrocompetent DH10B E. coli cells containing BAC
CC   of intereset. Shake bacteria 1 h at 30 deg C in 1ml SOC-medium
CC   without antibiotics. Plate 100µl at an agar plate with kanamycin and
CC   the selection for the target DNA. Spin the remaining 900 µL down;
CC   resuspend in 100 µl and plate at a 2nd agar plate. Incubate over
CC   night at 41-43 deg C.Check colonies for cointegrates with miniprep
CC   and restriction (growth at 43°C!!!) Save the cointegrates at a new
CC   agar plate with kanamycin and your BAC's selection marker
CC   (chloramphenicol [CAM]) at 41-43 deg C. Resolution of cointegrates:
CC   Grow a single (fresh) colony 3-4 hours to overnight in LB only with
CC   selection for your target BAC at 37 and/or 41 deg C. Then streak or
CC   plate 1-3 µL on 1-3 agar plates containing selection for your target
CC   BAC (chloramphenicol) plus 5-10% sucrose (use fresh sucrose LB
CC   plates). Incubate overnight at 37 deg C. Replica plate large
CC   colonies on separate kanamycin and BAC selectable marker plates
CC   (chloramphenicol). Incubate overnight at 32-37 deg C. Some of the
CC   colonies should only grow on plates without kanamycin. These are
CC   your resolved co-integrates.
